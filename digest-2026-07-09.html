We Heard From More Than 1,000 Readers on State Farm's Controversial AI Makeover

After State Farm's AI overhaul, roughly 900-1,000 readers weighed in with reactions ranging from "terrible" and "infuriating" to "it sucks," describing chatbots and automated systems, though some accepted AI as inevitable if it kept prices low. China Says It Has Found Security Vulnerabilities in Anthropic's Claude Code

China's National Vulnerability Database warned on July 8, 2026 that Anthropic's Claude Code contains a "backdoor" that transmits user location and identity data to remote servers without consent, affecting versions 2.1.91 through 2.1.196 released between April and June, and urged users to uninstall or upgrade immediately. The dispute centers on whether the tracking mechanism is a genuine security vulnerability or a legitimate anti-piracy measure, reflecting broader U.S.-China AI tensions.

Will Someone Finally Blink in the AI Spending War?

Google, Amazon, Microsoft, and Meta collectively plan to spend around $725 billion on capital expenditures in 2026, up roughly 77% from 2025's record $410 billion, with the five largest hyperscalers projected to spend $700 billion to $900 billion overall. Roughly 75%, or about $450 billion, is tied directly to AI infrastructure—servers, GPUs, and datacenters—pushing capital intensity to historically unthinkable levels, with some firms dedicating 45-57% of revenue to infrastructure. Analysts note that pulling back now carries its own catastrophic competitive risks in an increasingly "Darwinian" race, meaning few executives are willing to blink first. Goldman Sachs projects a combined $5.3 trillion in capex across the four largest hyperscalers from fiscal 2025 through 2030.

Private-Equity Firms Are Sitting on a Nine-Year Backlog

Buyout firms would need roughly nine years to clear their inventory of unsold portfolio companies at the current exit pace, according to a PwC analysis of PitchBook data. About 13,500 U.S. companies sat in PE portfolios as of June 30, 2026, with nearly 4,000 held six-plus years and about 1,500 held nine or more years; Bain & Company pegged the global buyout backlog at roughly 32,000 companies worth about $3.8 trillion. World's Hottest Market Risks Becoming a Squid Game

The index fell more than 5% on Wednesday, July 8, leaving it about 20% below its June 19 record high, as the chip-fueled rally cracked. 3 Questions to Ask Yourself About Your First 401(k)

When opening a first 401(k), advisers say the three questions that matter most are whether you're contributing enough to capture the full employer match, whether to choose Roth (after-tax) or traditional (pre-tax) contributions, and how your money is invested and vested. Not contributing enough to earn a match — commonly a 100% match up to 4%, or 50% up to 6% — effectively leaves free compensation on the table. The Roth-versus-traditional choice hinges on whether you expect a higher tax rate in retirement than today, since Roth withdrawals are tax-free while traditional contributions defer taxes now. Vesting schedules matter too: your own contributions are always 100% yours, but employer matches may take years to fully vest, so leaving early can forfeit that money.

Sources: Indeed, Charles Schwab, Empower

A Couple Living on One Income Wants to Buy a Home. Can They Afford It?

The standard benchmark for single-income home affordability in 2026 remains the 28/36 rule: keep total housing costs (principal, interest, taxes, insurance and HOA fees) at or below 28% of gross monthly income, and all debt payments under 36%. There is no fixed minimum income to qualify — lenders instead weigh debt-to-income ratio, employment stability, credit score and loan type, with most conventional loans requiring a credit score of at least 620 and down payments ranging from as little as 0% (VA) or 3.5% (FHA) to conventional minimums. Because one earner carries the entire mortgage, advisers stress building a robust emergency fund to cover repairs or a loss of income. The rules of thumb are only starting points: buyers should evaluate their full financial picture and whether a payment will still feel comfortable years down the road.

Sources: Opendoor, Loan Pronto, Freedom Mortgage
